Welcome to the Yedp Action Director! This powerful ComfyUI custom node acts as a miniature 3D engine directly inside your workflow. It allows you to pose 3D characters, sequence animations, apply facial motion capture, light scenes, and load environments (including Gaussian Splat .ply files).

When you are ready, the node "bakes" your scene into 7 perfectly aligned image batches (Pose, Depth, Canny, Normal, Shaded, Alpha, and Textured) ready to be routed directly into ControlNet.

1. Initial Setup & Node Resolution

Before diving into the 3D viewport, you control the core properties of your scene using the standard ComfyUI input widgets on the node itself.

  • width / height: Sets the exact pixel resolution of your final rendered passes. The internal 3D camera will automatically adjust its aspect ratio to match these dimensions.
  • frame_count: The total number of frames to bake for your animation.
  • fps: The framerate (Frames Per Second) used to calculate animation speed and motion capture playback.

2. The Main Interface & Viewport

The 3D Viewport is divided into three main areas: the Top Header Bar, the 3D Canvas, and the Bottom Timeline.

The Top Header Bar

This bar controls your viewport rendering modes, physical saving, and baking triggers.

  • Render Toggles: Switch how the viewport previews your scene.
    • Textured displays original colors/materials.
    • Shaded displays standard grey materials.
    • Depth previews the Z-Depth pass.
    • Skel toggles the visibility of the character bone helpers.
  • Depth Controls (NEAR / FAR): Only visible in Depth mode. These dictate the minimum and maximum clipping range of the depth map. Adjust these to get the best contrast in your depth output!
  • 💾 SAVE / 📂 LOAD: Safely save and restore your entire 3D scene setup directly to your hard drive. This bypasses ComfyUI's volatile workflow memory and prevents data loss if you accidentally delete the node. Saves are stored as `.json` files in the input/yedp_scenes/ folder.
  • ↻ SYNC FOLDERS: Refreshes the custom_nodes/ComfyUI-Yedp-Action-Director/web and input/yedp_... folders. Click this if you just added a new 3D model or animation to your folders and need it to show up in the menus.
  • BAKE FRAME / BAKE V9.3: Triggers the rendering process. Bake Frame renders only the current frame, while Bake V9.3 renders the entire frame count.

The Timeline

Located at the bottom of the viewport.

  • Play/Pause Button: Previews your animation loop in real-time.
  • Scrub Slider: Allows you to manually scrub through the animation frame-by-frame.

3. Navigation & Gizmos

Navigating the Action Director will feel familiar if you use tools like Maya or Unity.

Camera Navigation

  • Left-Click + Drag: Orbit the camera around the target.
  • Right-Click + Drag: Pan/Track the camera.
  • Scroll Wheel: Zoom in and out.

Transform Gizmos & Hotkeys

When you click on a Character, Light, or Environment prop, a transformation gizmo will appear. You can change the current selection position/rotation/scale and the gizmo mode using the floating widget in the top left, or by using keyboard hotkeys:

  • G: Translate (Move)
  • R: Rotate
  • S: Scale (Available for Characters and Environments)

View Navigation Panel

In the top right of the viewport is a quick-navigation menu. Use this to instantly snap your camera to the Top, Bottom, Left, Right, Front, or Back of your focal point. The Reset button returns the camera to the default starting position.

4. The Sidebar Categories

The right sidebar houses all the specific controls for objects in your scene.

📍 Transform

Displays the precise numerical Transform (Position, Rotation, Scale) of your currently selected object. You can type exact values here for precision adjustments.

🎥 Camera

Controls your main rendering camera.

  • Ortho Toggle: Switches the camera between Perspective and Orthographic modes.
  • FOV: Adjusts the Field of View for the perspective camera.
  • Keyframing (Set Start / Set End): Allows you to create a simple camera sweep. Move the camera to your starting position and click "Set Start", then move it to the end position and click "Set End". Use the easing dropdown (e.g., easeInOut) to smooth the motion.
  • Animation Import: You can load .fbx or .glb files containing camera animation data from the dropdown.
  • Override Toggle: Checking this forces the viewport to look directly through your imported, animated camera.
  • Fix Transform (Rx, Ry, Rz, Scl): If your imported camera faces the wrong axis (common with FBX imports from different software), use these offsets to correct it.

💡 Lighting

Add custom lighting to affect the Shaded and Textured render passes.

  • + Add Light: Spawns a new light source.
  • Type: Switch between Ambient, Directional, Point, and Spot lights.
  • Color / Int (Intensity): Adjust the look of the light.
  • Rng (Range) / Ang (Angle): Specific controls for Point and Spot lights.
  • Shadows Toggle: Enable or disable shadow casting for this specific light.

🧍 Characters

The core of the Action Director.

  • Model Dropdown & + Add Char: Select a base .glb or .fbx rig from your web folder and click add.
  • Rig Swapping: Inside the character card, you can change the "Model" dropdown to seamlessly replace the rig geometry while retaining all animations and transforms.
  • Animation Sequence: You can queue multiple animations to play one after another! Click "+ Add Sequence Anim" to add slots.
  • Blend (s): Sets the auto-crossfade duration (in seconds) between your sequential animation clips.
  • Face Scale: Modifies the internal mapping distance for facial motion capture, ensuring expressions fit different character proportions.
  • M / F Toggle: Switches the depth-map output between the Male and Female depth-meshes bound to your rig.
  • Face Toggle: Hides or shows the face mesh in the OpenPose render pass.
  • Loop vs. Root Motion: Loop repeats the animation sequence infinitely. Root M. (Root Motion) disables snapping and integrates the character's forward movement continuously through 3D space.

🎭 Motion Capture

Drive character facial expressions using your webcam or a pre-recorded video file!

  • Source: Choose Webcam for live capture, or Video File to upload a local video.
  • Video Trim Slider: When using a Video File, a dual-handle range slider appears. This allows you to trim exactly which segment of the video you want to process, saving time and memory!
  • Capture Name: Before recording or processing, you can type a custom name into the text field so it's easier to identify in your dropdown menus later.
  • Start / Rec: Click Start to initialize MediaPipe. Click Rec to capture the motion.
  • Note for Video: Recording a video file happens offline sequentially, ensuring perfect 30fps tracking with zero dropped frames.
  • Save: Saves the tracked facial data to your local `/input/yedp_mocap/` folder as a reusable JSON file.
  • Face Rig Bind (+ Add Face Bind): This connects saved Mocap data to a character in your scene. Select your Target Character, select the Mocap file, and adjust the Amp (Amplitude) slider to exaggerate or dampen the facial movements.

⛰️ Environments

Load custom 3D sets, props, or Point Clouds.

  • + Add Env: Creates a new environment container.
  • Model Selection: Dropdown reads from your `input/yedp_envs` folder. Supports .glb, .gltf, .fbx, .obj, and .ply.
  • Gaussian Splats (.ply): The Action Director natively supports Gaussian Splat point clouds! When you load a .ply file, it will render its original vertex colors in the Textured pass, and automatically cast dense, accurate shadows and depth-maps using a custom internal shader. You can easily toggle whether a `.ply` file renders as standard points or as a Gaussian Splat using the checkbox (if the checkbox isn't visible click on the "Sync Folders" button).
  • Loop (Anim): If your imported environment contains an animation (like a spinning fan or moving platform), this toggles whether it loops.

5. Baking & Outputs

When your scene is ready, click BAKE V9.3 at the top right of the viewport.

The engine will lock the UI, compress the canvas, and render your frame count perfectly. Once it finishes uploading to the ComfyUI backend, the node will output 7 individual image batches that you can route into standard nodes:

  • POSE_BATCH: Standard OpenPose skeleton colors.
  • DEPTH_BATCH: Z-Depth map for depth ControlNets.
  • CANNY_BATCH: A matcap rim-light render optimized for edge detection.
  • NORMAL_BATCH: World-space Normal map.
  • SHADED_BATCH: Standard grey diffuse render with shadows.
  • ALPHA_BATCH: Pure black and white silhouette mask.
  • TEXTURED_BATCH: Full-color render using original materials and point-cloud vertex colors.

Folder Structure Reference

To ensure your files appear in the Action Director's dropdown menus, place them in the following directories:

Rigs: custom_nodes/ComfyUI-Yedp-Action-Director/web/ (.glb, .fbx)
Animations: input/yedp_anims/ (.glb, .fbx, .bvh)
Environments: input/yedp_envs/ (.glb, .fbx, .obj, .ply)
Cameras: input/yedp_cams/ (.glb, .fbx)
Mocap Data: input/yedp_mocap/ (.json)
Saved Scenes: input/yedp_scenes/ (.json)
